Getting Here

The Comox Valley is located on Vancouver Island, British Columbia. Easy access is possible by car, boat, or air.

Arriving by Car

You can drive to the Comox Valley after taking a ferry from mainland British Columbia. Take the BC Ferry to Nanaimo from Tsawwassen or Horseshoe Bay terminals. At the Nanaimo terminus, travel north on highway 19 to the Comox Valley, exit 117, or on highway 19A to Courtenay. The Comox Valley is about 100 km, (62 miles), north of Nanaimo.

You can also drive north from Victoria after taking the Black Ball Ferry. The Comox Valley is about 225 km, (140 miles), north of Victoria.

Arriving by Air

The Comox Valley is served by YQQ connections to Vancouver, Edmonton and Calgary. WestJet and Air Canada operate daily flights. Travel time is 20 minutes from Vancouver, about an hour from Calgary or Edmonton.

Float plane service is also offered by West Coast Air from Vancouver, (direct daily), and Kenmore Air from Seattle, (check carrier for details).

Arriving by Boat

The Comox Valley is served by several marinas offering protected transient and longer-term moorage. Comox Harbour is located on the west side of Georgia Straight on the Inland water route. Marine services, including divers and mechanics, are available from Will's Marine Supply.
