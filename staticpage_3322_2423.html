Parks

The Comox Valley contains a large number of parks, with a huge variety of terrains. From the ocean, to the forests, to the mountains, you will find incredible parks across the area. You can enjoy many outdoor activities such as fishing, hiking, camping, and more. There are specific areas for mountain biking, motocross, horseback riding, and more. Just check them out!

Strathcona Park

Located adjacent to the Mount Washington Ski Resort, this large park boasts scenery, hiking, and fishing opportunities that are rarely matched anywhere. Strathcona was the first dedicated provincial park and is one of the largest parks in BC.

Seal Bay Park

Located in northeast, rural Courtenay, Seal Bay Park is the largest municipal park in the Comox Valley. It contains many walking trails, some which are wheelchair accessible. This park contains specific areas for hiking, biking, horseback riding. Wilderness abounds from the sea to the forests and the included marsh.

Nymph Falls

This park is located on the Puntledge River. Nymph Falls contains a large network of trails which connect to a system. Visitors can easily hike from this park to Comox Lake along a well travelled path. Located on Forbidden Plateau Road, it is about 15 minutes outside of Courtenay.

Goose Spit Park

Located just east of Comox, Goose Spit is a sandy peninsula that protects Comox Harbour. Visitors can easily walk to the park. A recent addition has been the set of stairs that climb to a nearby road. Fabulous panoramic views of the Comox Valley, mountains, and the nearby islands Denman and Hornby. Low tide offers visitors the opportunity to sunbathe on the beach. The protected inside waters are frequented by sailboards and small sailboats.
